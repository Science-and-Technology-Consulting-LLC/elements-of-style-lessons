# Who this site is for

## Specifically, we're writing for…

- **People who have to deliver results on a deadline** and don't have
  time to detour into infrastructure for its own sake. Every tool here
  earns its place by saving you time later.
- **People who move between platforms** — your laptop, ADAPTS, the All of Us
  Workbench, Biowulf, a CAVATICA project, a JupyterHub somewhere. The
  lessons are designed so the muscle memory transfers.
- **People at every skill level**, from "this is my first terminal
  session" to "I've shipped Nextflow pipelines to production." The early
  lessons are gentle on purpose; the later ones still have something for
  the experts.

## Principles we hold ourselves to

1. **Educate without disrupting.** We never contradict a director or a
   platform constraint. We add tools you can reach for when the
   opportunity arises.
2. **Show, don't just tell.** Every page has runnable code or a real
   artefact. No theory without practice.
3. **Honour the constraints.** When a platform can't `git pull`, we offer
   bridge patterns. When a workflow has to copy/paste, we make copy/paste
   reproducible.
4. **Be warm.** Working scientists already have enough sources of
   stress. This site is allowed to sound like a person.

## What this site is *not*

- A complete reference for any one tool. We point you to the official
  docs liberally.
- A prescription. The right way to work is the way that ships your
  science. We offer suggestions, not rules.
